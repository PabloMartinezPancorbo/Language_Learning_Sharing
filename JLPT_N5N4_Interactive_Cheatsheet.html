The 3 Types of Japanese Verbs

To master Japanese, you must know how to conjugate verbs into Dictionary form, Masu-form (polite), and Te-form (connecting). Japanese verbs are split into 3 groups based on their dictionary form endings.

Group 1: Godan Verbs (U-Verbs)

These end in u, tsu, ru, mu, nu, bu, ku, gu, su. To make them polite (-masu), change the 'u' sound to an 'i' sound.

Dictionary (Plain)RomajiPolite (-masu)Te-FormMeaning
書くkaku書きます (kakimasu)書いて (kaite)To write
飲むnomu飲みます (nomimasu)飲んで (nonde)To drink
話すhanasu話します (hanashimasu)話して (hanashite)To speak
行くiku行きます (ikimasu)行って (itte) *Irregular teTo go

Group 2: Ichidan Verbs (Ru-Verbs)

These end in -iru or -eru. They are the easiest! Just drop the 'ru' and add the suffix.

Dictionary (Plain)RomajiPolite (-masu)Te-FormMeaning
食べるtaberu食べます (tabemasu)食べて (tabete)To eat
見るmiru見ます (mimasu)見て (mite)To see/watch
起きるokiru起きます (okimasu)起きて (okite)To wake up

Group 3: Irregular Verbs

There are only two main irregular verbs you must memorize entirely.

Dictionary (Plain)RomajiPolite (-masu)Te-FormMeaning
するsuruします (shimasu)して (shite)To do
来るkuru来ます (kimasu)来て (kite)To come
